L4 special-issue retrieval/geometry simulation
================================================

Purpose
-------
Relate finite-size geometric Hebbian writing costs to Hopfield retrieval
reliability and compare simulated costs with an overlap-moment prediction.

Executed design
---------------
N = 128, 256, 512
P/N = 0.05, 0.10, 0.15, 0.20, 0.30
generators = latent, block, mixture
correlation parameters = 0.15, 0.25, 0.35, 0.45
cue-corruption probabilities = 0.05, 0.10, 0.15, 0.20
50 realizations; 12 cues per noise level; 25 update steps
lambda = 2; retrieval success threshold = final overlap >= 0.90
seed = 20260715

Scale
-----
18,000 memory-realization rows and 864,000 noisy-cue trials.
The correlated-minus-uncorrelated analysis contains 720 condition contrasts.

Corrected analytical benchmark
------------------------------
The active script includes the complete fourth-moment coefficient for the
(3,1) and (2,1,1) index partitions. The earlier internal output is retained
only in revision_history for auditability. The active comparison over 180
conditions gives approximately:

Pearson correlation       0.99917
Spearman correlation      0.99271
Mean prediction bias     -8.1e-7
Mean absolute error       1.62e-5

Retrieval-risk interpretation
-----------------------------
Across all 720 contrasts, the excess intensive cost is a moderate statistical
predictor of increased retrieval-failure risk (Spearman about 0.407), but a
weaker predictor of continuous final-overlap loss (Spearman about 0.229).
For nominal loads P/N <= 0.20, the failure-risk association is stronger
(Spearman about 0.552). These are diagnostic associations, not evidence for
a new learning algorithm or deterministic prediction of individual recalls.

Checkpoint and resume
---------------------
Each completed realization is recorded in data/completed_tasks.txt. Re-running
the same command resumes from unfinished tasks. Use --restart to erase prior
CSV/checkpoint outputs and begin again.
